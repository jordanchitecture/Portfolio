LOST OBJECT

Exploring the functionality of JavaScript coding, I used shape primitives to create a visual representation of an item that is frequently lost, Apple Airpods.

PARAMETRIC FISH

To create a fish that responded to the users mouse location, I utilized parametric coding allowing different parameters to be changed depending on the users mouse coordinates.

EYE ON YOU

With subtle undertones of online surveillance and how every move you make online is being recorded to some capacity, I crafted a parametric sequence that tracks the users mouse movements and clicks which is then followed by an ominous eye that appears after all three rings are closed. CLICK!

HYBRID MONSTER

Collaborating with 2 other creators, we each designed a separate creature and, from those different parts, assembled our own hybrid monster. This project involved exploring the use of SVG files in JavaScript.

OPTICAL ILLUSION

Drawing inspiration from my lineage and traditional Norwegian patterns and motifs, I designed an optical illusion that responds to the location and clicking of the mouse to create different patterns and illusions. CLICK!

DATA PORTRAIT

Recording data on different factors based on the occurrences of when I looked out a window over the course of a weekend, I coded a scene in which the diorama reflects the data I collected. This includes weather conditions, time of day, and if I was listening to music at that moment. CLICK!

GETTIN' LOST

To end off my coding endeavors for the semester, I wanted to take on an ambitious project of coding a Mario inspired game. Gettingâ€™ Lost is based on a real life experience of me attempting to get to the New York Public Library but getting lost along the way. In this game which only contains the first level, you will play as me running through Brooklyn making it to the subway stop at the end of the level while attempting to collect as many MetroCards as you can. The level starts at the front gates of Pratt Institute and moves through the brownstones of Clinton Hill, Fort Greene Park, past the Brooklyn Academy of Music, through Downtown Brooklyn, and ending at the subway station in front of Brooklyn Borough Hall.
